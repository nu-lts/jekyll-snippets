  • The secret of a great success for which you are at a loss to account is a crime that has never been found out, because it was properly executed.
"Behind every great fortune is a crime" is amply proved in Frank Partnoy's sharp indictment, Infectious Greed. The book was published in 2003, and I was motivated to read it in the wake of the financial crisis, by the reprint of this review in Salon. (link)  Partnoy's book is long, This recent NY Times article about Lehman Brothers' use of a shadow altar ego company underscores that this is still a relatively current practice. And Goldman Sachs Partnoy's recommendations:
  1. Treat derivatives like other financial instruments
  2. Shift from rules to standards
  3. Eliminate the oligopoly lock of gatekeepers, especially credit-rating agencies
  4. Prosecute complex financial fraud
  5. Encourage informed investors to bet against stocks (maybe also mention the dangers--ie. what Paulson is doing)
  6. Encourage investors to control and monitor their own investments
