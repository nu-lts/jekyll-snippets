The FDLP is a program that disseminates U.S. Government information to the American public through libraries across the nation. As one of two selective depositories for the district, Snell Library receives forty-five percent of the federal publication series available to depository libraries. It chooses the governmental material most applicable to the University and the surrounding populace it serves. Much of this material is now sent via the internet, but written publications and disks are also sent to Northeastern. In a review of Snell Library in FDLP's Depository Library Spotlight, Snell was commended for the easy access and visibility of the content in its depository:

"Snell Library does not simply meet the minimum legal and program requirements for promoting access to their depository collection. To the contrary, the library makes every effort to ensure not only that the depository collection is visible, but that all users are welcome to access tangible and online materials alike. For any library to embrace the spirit of the Federal Depository Library Program to the extent that Snell has is commendable. All depositories looking to emphasize free access by all users need only follow the example set by the Snell Library at Northeastern University."
