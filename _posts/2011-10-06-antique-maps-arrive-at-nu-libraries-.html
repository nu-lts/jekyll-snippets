--- layout: post title: Antique maps arrive at NU Libraries! categories: - Serendipity tags: [] status: pending type: post published: false meta: _edit_last: '113' --- Quite recently, the well-known New York owner of Arader Galleries, Graham Arader, has made available to the University a number of antique framed prints and maps. These one-of-a-kind pieces are filled with colorful outlines and unique drawings, which makes the prints and maps even more aesthetically pleasing.

The collection is fortunately available for public viewing as it is also incorporated into the course, 'Picturing the World,' taught by Dean Georges Van Den Abbeele of CSSH this fall. The course gears towards introducing freshmen to the natural world and technological breakthrough that enabled early modern printing used for books, art prints, and maps.

Come see these historical Graham Arader maps for yourself! They are located on the second floor of the Library right outside the staff lounge as well as in and along the hallway leading into the 295 classroom.
