--- layout: post title: Celebrate Native American Heritage Month! categories: - Serendipity tags: [] status: pending type: post published: false meta: _edit_last: '113' --- With the craze of midterm week gradually dying down, students can now look forward to relaxing in November. However, what many students may not know about the month of November is that it marks the festive celebration of Native American Heritage Month, which aims to highlight the establishment, growth, and exceptional work that Native Americans have carried out over the past hundreds of years. Students can honor this month by checking out one of the many resources Snell Library offers to educate Northeastern University on the extensive contributions Native Americans have made. Here are some examples that could be found right here at Snell! The novel, Tribal Theory in Native American Literature, by Penelope Myrtle Kelsey explores how one might implement tribal knowledge into theoretical framework when reading the text of Native Americans. Reading Native American Women: Critical/Creative Representations edited by Inés Hernandes-Avila emphasizes the intellectual and innovative work that Native American women today are doing. Through their political, cultural, and creative statements, these women are spotlighted on their strength and determination. Are you more into music? No worries! Listen to the mesmerizing music of the Native American culture by checking out Native American Music in Eastern North America: Experiencing Music, Expressing Culture by Beverley Diamond. To find more resources that Snell Library has pertaining to Native American heritage, check out Northeastern's very own library catalog, Nucat!
