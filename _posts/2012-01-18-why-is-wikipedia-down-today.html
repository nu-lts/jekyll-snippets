(Jan. 18, 2012) categories: - Information and Society - Library News and Events - Research Online - Scholarly Communications - Tech Alerts tags: - copyright - Faculty - intellectual freedom - Internet - Internet Archive - piracy - research - Students - wikipedia status: publish type: post published: true meta: _edit_last: '49' --- Major websites such as Wikipedia and the Internet Archive are holding blackouts today, January 18, 2012, in protest of two anti-piracy bills currently before Congress. Many believe that the Stop Online Piracy Act (SOPA) and the Protect Intellectual Property Act (PIPA) go too far in their efforts to curb illegal downloading and streaming of movies and television shows. The stated intent of these acts is to protect the intellectual property rights of copyright holders (sometimes the authors or creators but more frequently the large media corporations who own the works). However, if passed into law, they may set a dangerous precedent for permitting private companies to block access to information. Wikipedia, among other sites, has chosen to do just that today - block access to the information that millions of us seek every day - in order to highlight what they feel could happen if SOPA and PIPA are passed.
