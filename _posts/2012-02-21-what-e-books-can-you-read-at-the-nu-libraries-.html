--- layout: post title: What e-books can you read at the NU Libraries? categories: - Serendipity tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: _edit_last: '1' --- Did you get a Kindle, Nook, or, lucky you, an iPad for the holidays?  Have you been wondering if you can just download something fun to read on the bus, using the library? Well, you can! If you want information about what you can do with different devices, see our E-books and Devices page. And keep this in mind:
  1. If you like to read non-fiction (handbooks, essays, biography), there's plenty to read. There's not much e-book fiction available yet through Northeastern.
  2. The brave new world of digital rights management means that the first time you download a library e-book, you'll need to install software and set up an account.
Here's what I'm currently reading: Denis Noble, The music of life about the definition of "life" in the age biogenetics.  I'm reading this now on an iPhone 3 from ebrary and all I can say is the material is fairly dense and intense for my itty bitty smartphone screen. Vintage Amis: a collection of essays by Martin Amis, the great English writer, downloaded to my Nook.  The essays are pretty light nonfiction, and just about right for an e-reading device. Here are some other gems available electronically: Walmart in China a collection of essays about the relationship between the nation-state superpower and the retail superpower (ebrary) The Definitive Guide to Drupal 7 (Apress, Benjamin Melacon)  (safari, online) Secondary STEM education reform edited by Carla Johnson, this collection of essays discusses how to encourage study of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics in high schools.  available from ebrary, For a list of all our eb00k providers, see our Guide to ebook collections at Snell Library.
